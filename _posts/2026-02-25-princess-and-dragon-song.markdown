A Ligvar song describing human sacrifices.


## Lingvar version

Peltis kaks akrintis skrelamas<br>
Ir veitis gal guddtis athamas<br>
Tor veitise akrintis ortun<br>
Har neikats karame lin gestun<br>

Otatis beltisint kastamas<br>
Badis musis, kva badis galas?<br>
Vedingotis östals talame<br>
Asaratals mitis merame<br>

Vedingotis akrinth turamas<br>
Tra ilge longs, tra veis athas<br>
Fian rugans veitis, kvi brost ortint<br>
(Har) neikats anen eistraide kraint<br>


## English translation

Birds sweep over the mountains.<br>
Across the meadows, pale flowers quicken into spring.<br>
Facing the meadows lies a mountain lake<br>
there the Insatiable waits for a victim.<br>

The daughters sift through ashes,<br>
a black lot - or a white?<br>
The prince’s daughter dons a cloak of gold;<br>
the girl tries on a cloak of tears.<br>
  
The prince’s daughter is taken through the hills,<br>
along a long road, across a springtime meadow.<br>
Beyond the will-o’-wisps’ meadows, where a footbridge spans the lake,<br>
the Insatiable devours her on the shore’s edge.<br>
